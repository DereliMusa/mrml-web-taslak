Thermoelectric Performance Enhancement via Nanostructuring in BiSbTe Alloys

Advanced Energy Materials • Aralik 2025 • DOI: 10.1002/aenm.202401234

Yazarlar
Ahmet Yilmaz Zeynep Arslan Arda Koc
Ozet

Abstract

Thermoelectric materials capable of directly converting waste heat to electricity have attracted significant attention for sustainable energy applications. In this study, we report the synthesis and characterization of nanostructured BiSbTe alloys with enhanced thermoelectric performance. By employing a combination of ball milling and spark plasma sintering, we achieved grain sizes in the range of 50-200 nm, which significantly suppressed lattice thermal conductivity.

The optimal composition Bi0.4Sb1.6Te3 with 0.5 wt% Se doping demonstrated a peak ZT value of 1.85 at 323 K, representing a 35% improvement over the bulk counterpart. The enhancement is attributed to the synergistic effect of nanostructuring-induced phonon scattering and optimized carrier concentration through chemical doping. These results suggest that the proposed approach holds considerable promise for near-room-temperature waste heat recovery applications.
